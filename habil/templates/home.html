About TanAir

Our main goal is to provide real-time Air pollution data and prediction for transformative decision Making

The Electronics and Telecommunication Department at the Dar es Salaam Institute of Technology (DIT) is dedicated to providing high-quality education and innovative research in the fields of electronics, telecommunication, and related technologies. Our department fosters a culture of academic excellence and creativity, equipping students with the knowledge and skills needed to tackle real-world challenges.

The project, TanAir, is an effort to address the increasing concerns of air pollution in urban settings including Dar es Salaam. With a focus on leveraging IoT and AI technologies, our aim is to develop a real-time air quality monitoring system that can predict and manage pollution levels, helping improve the health and well-being of residents in Dar es Salaam. Led by students Habil Khamisi Said and Godwin Silvery Aruga, under the guidance of Dr. Mbazingwa E. Mkiramweni, this project reflects our commitment to solving environmental challenges through technological innovation.

Key Features Leveraged by TanAir System

Real-Time Pollution Monitoring

TanAir allows for continuous, real-time monitoring of pollution to provide instant feedback on cognitive performance and forecast future trends.

Air Monitoring Dashboard

The system provides a web-based interface for monitoring pollution trends and optimizing decision-making, offering statistical-based data to enhance clarity and reduce stress.

AI-Powered Insights

TanAir integrates AI algorithms to analyze data and provide actionable insights for optimizing pollution control and well-being.

Forecast of Pollution Trend

The system offers prediction of air pollution trends based on historical patterns, ensuring targeted improvements in pollution management and its performance.
